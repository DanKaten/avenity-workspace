A Three-Year Compounding Story

The boring SEO work we started in 2023 is why both Google and ChatGPT now name Easley Enterprises first.

No rebrands. No paid lift. No tricks. Just three years of fundamentals, done consistently — and a local janitorial company that now gets cited by every AI surface a customer might ask.

The Short Version

In 2023 we built Easley Enterprises a new website and put them on a steady, no-frills SEO program. Three years later — with no aggressive ad spend and no special "AI" strategy — Easley is the first company named in Google's AI Overview and the first company recommended by ChatGPT when someone searches for a janitorial provider in Montgomery County. That's what compounding looks like.

01 — The SetupAn older, established local business that needed a foundation that could last.

Easley Enterprises is one of the most established commercial janitorial and property maintenance providers in Montgomery County. When they came to us in 2023, they didn't need a flashy rebrand — they needed a digital foundation that matched the quality of the operation they'd already built. We rebuilt the website with clean architecture, then put them on a deliberate, monthly local-SEO cadence and left it alone to do its work.

What we explicitly didn't do: chase trends, retool the strategy every quarter, pile on extra services to bill more, or pitch them on "AI optimization" before AI optimization was even a category. We did the textbook things and kept doing them.

02 — The WorkFour boring categories. Done every month for three years.

  • On-Page & Technical SEO Site speed, crawlability, schema, title tags, internal linking, sitemap hygiene. The unsexy fundamentals that most agencies do once and forget.
  • Google Business Profile Categories, service areas, GBP posts, review cadence, photo updates. Treated like a living asset, not a set-and-forget listing.
  • Local Citations & Directories NAP consistency across the directories that local-search algorithms still trust. Done right once, audited regularly.
  • Content & Blog Publishing A consistent publishing cadence on the topics buyers actually research — not keyword filler. Topical authority built one post at a time.

That's it. That is the whole engagement. No black-box link schemes, no PR pushes, no expensive production. The reason this case study is interesting is precisely because the inputs are so ordinary.

03 — The ResultCited first on every AI surface a buyer is likely to use.

In the spring of 2026, we ran the obvious queries a Montgomery County business owner would actually type when looking for a janitorial provider. Easley Enterprises didn't just rank — Easley got named. By the AI itself. On every major surface.

04 — Why This Matters For YouThe window for compounding is open right now. It won't be in two years.

Here's the thing about generative-AI search results: they are pulling from the same signals that Google has rewarded for a decade — clean technical foundations, consistent local presence, real content, real reviews, real authority. The companies showing up in AI Overviews and ChatGPT and Grok answers in 2026 are the companies that did the boring work in 2023, 2024, and 2025.

You can't shortcut your way into this. There's no AI-specific button to push. The businesses that will be cited as the answer in 2028 and 2030 are the ones that start now and stay consistent. Easley's story isn't impressive because we did something clever. It's impressive because we did the right things, and didn't stop.

The companies winning AI search aren't the ones with the cleverest 2026 strategy. They're the ones who quietly did the right thing every month for three straight years. — The Avenity Business Solutions point of view

05 — TakeawaysFour lessons for any local business owner reading this.

01 — Start sooner than you think you need to

The compounding curve is real. The work you start today won't pay off this quarter — it will pay off three years from now, when AI is even more central to how customers find providers.

02 — Boring beats clever

On-page, GBP, citations, content. That's it. If your agency is selling you exotic tactics before they've nailed the fundamentals, you're paying for the wrong thing.

03 — Consistency is the actual moat

Anyone can do one quarter of good SEO. Almost no one does twelve consecutive quarters of it. That gap is where category-defining local visibility gets built.

04 — AI surfaces reward authority, not novelty

Google's AI Overview, ChatGPT, and Grok all pulled from established signals: longevity, citations, content, reviews. The "AI SEO" playbook is mostly the regular SEO playbook, executed with patience.

If you want a three-year story like this, the best time to start was 2023.
The second-best time is now.
